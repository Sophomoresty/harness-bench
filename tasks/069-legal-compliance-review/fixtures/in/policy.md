# Marketing Compliance Policy

CLAIM-1: Do not promise guaranteed outcomes, universal results, risk-free performance, or omnipotent automation ("always converts", "never fails", "every single time").

CLAIM-2: Do not imply regulatory approval unless **evidence_manifest.json** marks the supporting clearance file as present. This task ships with **`fda_clearance_document_present`: false**, so FDA-style wording is out of scope.

CLAIM-3: Health or medical claims such as cure, diagnosis, or treatment are prohibited for commerce analytics products.

CLAIM-4: Comparative claims must be supportable and should use qualified language. If **evidence_manifest.json** marks **published comparative study** absent, keep comparatives qualitative per this policy.

CLAIM-5: Do not remove benign product-context uses of regulated words when they are not making a performance, regulatory, or medical claim (schema labels, migration field names shown as technical identifiers).

CLAIM-6: Do not borrow clinical or therapeutic framing ("clinic-approved", diagnostic tone, wellness cure pathways) for non-medical commerce software—even metaphorically—unless clearly disclosed as non-medical analogy.

CLAIM-7: Promotional pricing language must not promise perpetual zero ongoing fees unless the renewal carve-out appears in the same visible surface with comparable prominence.

INTERNAL-1: Internal escalation notes, sales demos, or stakeholder urgency **never** waive CLAIM obligations. When guidance conflicts with policy, document the conflict and follow the policy clauses above.
